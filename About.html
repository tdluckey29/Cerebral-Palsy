What is Cerebral Palsy

Cerebral Palsy is a condition that effects, a person’s movement and motor skills. One of the main causes of CP is when the brain is damaged doing or before birth. Symptoms can also start to develop between the ages of 3 to 5. There are three types of cerebral palsy conditions, spastic, dyskinetic, and ataxic cerebral palsy. A person with spastic would have stiffness in their muscles, as well as difficult with movement. Dyskinetic, unless spastic will cause uncontrollable movements. Ataxic causes issues, with balance and eye sight.

What are the causes and symptoms

The causes of cerebral palsy are still unknown, but doctors have found these to be some of the main causes:

  • Bleeding in the brain during, before, or after birth
  • Seizures at birth or in the first month of life
  • Traumatic brain injures

Some of the symptoms of cerebral palsy include:

  • Unable to walk or crawl
  • Delopment Delay such as sitting up or reaching for toys by an appropriate age
  • Uncoordinated movements
